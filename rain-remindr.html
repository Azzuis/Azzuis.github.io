Development Log

March 2026 — Entry 003
Enclosure Prototype Complete

The first physical enclosure is off the printer. We went with a frosted white PLA to diffuse the LED light more evenly — the effect is exactly what we were after. Soft, ambient, impossible to ignore but never intrusive. Next up: weatherproofing tests before we finalize the design.

Hardware
February 2026 — Entry 002
Weather API Integration

Landed on Open-Meteo for our weather data source — it's free, accurate to the hour, and doesn't require an API key for basic use. The device now polls precipitation probability every 15 minutes and maps it to LED intensity. 30% chance? A gentle pulse. 80%? It glows hard. You can't miss it.

Software
January 2026 — Entry 001
The Idea Clicks

It started with a wet jacket. The idea is stupid simple: a small LED device that lives by your door. If rain is likely when you're due to leave, it glows. No app to check. No notification to dismiss. Just a light — the way a lamp tells you it's dark outside. We're building it.
